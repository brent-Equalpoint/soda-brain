# SODA Feedback Architecture

*Replacing the exit survey. Instrument what SODA can see. Ask only what it cannot. Make the asking pay the person who answers.*

**Version 1.0**

---

## 0. The thesis

A survey is a workaround for not having data. It asks people to do unpaid archival labor, at the moment of peak exhaustion, for a stranger's benefit. So you incentivize it, and the incentive manufactures the very bias you were trying to measure. You stop sampling attendees and start sampling people who wanted the gift card.

SODA does not have that problem, because **SODA was in the room.**

Three moves replace the survey:

1. **Instrument** what behavior already reveals. Do not ask what you can watch.
2. **Micro-ask** the irreducibly subjective, one tap, in the moment, about the specific thing.
3. **Exchange**, not extract. Turn the recap into something the person wants, and the data arrives as a byproduct.

Governing rule: **every question you add cuts your response rate.** Sixty percent of a shallow honest signal beats eight percent of a biased deep one.

---

## 1. Instrument: what SODA already knows without asking

None of the below requires a single question. It is all behavior, and behavior does not flatter itself.

### Participation
| Signal | What it tells you |
| --- | --- |
| Entered the room | Attendance, real, not registration |
| Time in room | Engagement depth |
| Built a card | Investment in being seen |
| Verified | Commitment threshold crossed |

### Connection quality (the heart of it)
| Signal | What it tells you |
| --- | --- |
| Connections made | Raw output of the room |
| **Considered vs. collected** | Did they write a "why," or rapid-add without engaging? This is the quality axis, and it is the one every other platform is blind to. |
| Intros started | Real conversation attempts, not just card grabs |
| Matches surfaced vs. matches acted on | Whether the matching is working or being ignored |

### The damning metric nobody surveys for
| Signal | What it tells you |
| --- | --- |
| **Entered but connected with nobody** | The single most important event health metric. These are the people who came, stood alone, and left. A survey never catches them; they do not fill it out. |
| Wandered without matching | The matching failed them, or the room composition was wrong |

### The honest judge: time
| Signal | What it tells you |
| --- | --- |
| **Warmth at 7 / 21 / 60 days** | Did the connection *survive*? |
| Connections still in rhythm | The only truthful answer to "was this event valuable" |
| Connections gone cold | Where the room produced contact but not relationship |

This is the competitive advantage. Every other platform asks people to *predict* value on the night, when nobody actually knows yet. SODA *measures* it retroactively, with time as the judge.

**Rule:** if a question can be answered by warmth data at 21 days, do not ask it at the exit.

## 2. Micro-ask: the irreducibly subjective

Some things behavior cannot see. Was the room welcoming? Was it too loud? Did you feel out of place? These need asking.

Principles for every micro-ask:
- **One question.** Not two.
- **In the moment**, attached to the thing it is about, not batched at the end.
- **A tap**, never typing.
- **Skippable, always.** Never blocks anything.
- **Never repeated** if ignored.

### The placements

| Moment | Question | Form | Why here |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Just added a connection | "Worth knowing?" | yes / no tap | Perfect recall, zero effort, already a moment about this person. Yields per-connection signal an exit survey can never produce. |
| Left the room (wrap-up) | "How did the room feel?" | 3 taps: Warm / Fine / Cold | One temperature reading on the venue and vibe, the thing behavior is blind to. |
| Recap (next day) | "Who should you reach out to first?" | tap a person | Priority signal for you, a to-do list for them. Value exchange, not extraction. |

That is the entire question budget. Three, spread across three moments, none of them a form.

---

## 3. Exchange: the recap that people want to fill out

The end-of-night touch is **not** a survey. It is the person's own memory, handed back to them, with an invitation to protect it.

**The screen says:** here are the people you met tonight. Add a note to anyone so you remember why.

**What the person gets:** future-them, three weeks from now, gets a warmth nudge that says "Maya, the one raising pre-seed for the gaming collab" instead of "Maya." They are doing it for themselves.

**What you get, as a byproduct:** the richest connection data available anywhere, freely given, unbiased by incentive. Who mattered. Why. Which connections were real and which were polite.

### The test for any question you want to add

> **Would this person do it even if you got nothing from it?**

If yes, it is a feature. If no, you are back to bribing, and the bribe will bias the answer. Every item in the recap must pass this test.

### What the recap must NOT do
- No rating scales.
- No "how would you rate this event."
- No required fields.
- No incentive, no raffle, no gift card. The moment you pay for the answer, you have changed who answers.

### Voice and text are equal paths, never fallbacks

Anything can happen in a real room. The mic gets denied, the phone is on a loud train, the model times out, the person simply does not want to talk out loud. **Voice is a convenience, never a requirement.**

| Condition | Behavior |
| --- | --- |
| Everything works | Mic and pencil offered side by side. Speak or type, both first class. |
| Mic blocked or unavailable | Mic button is not shown. An amber banner says typing works the same. Pencil is the primary path. No error screen. |
| Model down, slow, or fails validation | The **raw words are still saved.** Chips are simply absent and can be added later. The person loses nothing. |
| Person is mid-recording and changes their mind | "Type instead" is available during recording. |
| Nothing is entered | Skipping is always fine. The connection stays warm regardless. |

**Rules:**
- The pencil (text) is present in **every** state where the mic is present. It is never hidden behind a failure.
- A model failure degrades to plain text, never to an error. `why` without chips is a complete, useful note.
- No recap item is ever required. Skip is always visible.
- The screen never says "no typing needed," because typing must always be a real option.

**The principle, restated:** AI is never load-bearing for participation. If the model and the microphone both vanished tomorrow, the recap would still work, and the person would still get their memory back.

## 4. The one exit question (if you must have one)

If you want a single temperature check, make it **one tap** and accept that it is a temperature reading, not research.

Recommended: a would-you-recommend-to-a-friend style tap. It is the one question that is broadly predictive and costs two seconds.

Everything else you were going to put on that form is either measurable (§1) or better asked in-context (§2).

---

## 5. What you lose, and how to cover it

Instrumentation is not free of bias, and this doc would be dishonest not to say so.

**Behavior tells you what, not why.** The data shows a person made zero connections. It does not show that they felt unwelcome, or that the room was all founders and they were a job-seeker, or that the venue was so loud they gave up.

**The invisible person problem.** Someone who felt out of place and left early looks identical to a low-engagement row. They are the person you most need to hear from, and they will never fill out a survey either, so the survey was not catching them anyway.

**The cover: talk to actual humans.** Five real phone calls with attendees, especially the ones who connected with nobody, will surface more than five hundred survey rows. Instrument the *what*; talk to people about the *why*.

Recommendation: after each event, call three people who connected well and three who connected with nobody. Fifteen minutes each. That is the qualitative layer, and it cannot be automated.

## 6. Contract shapes

```json
// micro-ask, at connection
{
  "ask": "worth_knowing",
  "connection_id": "conn_maya",
  "answer": "yes | no | skipped",
  "asked_at": "in_context_connection"
}

// micro-ask, at wrap-up
{
  "ask": "room_feel",
  "event_id": "evt_lnt",
  "answer": "warm | fine | cold | skipped"
}

// recap note (the exchange, not a survey)
{
  "connection_id": "conn_maya",
  "why": "Raising pre-seed for the gaming collab",
  "source": "recap_voice | recap_typed",
  "for": "the person, not the operator"
}

// event health, derived, no asking
{
  "event_id": "evt_lnt",
  "entered": 200,
  "connected_zero": 24,          // the damning metric
  "avg_connections": 4.2,
  "considered_ratio": 0.68,      // wrote a why vs. rapid-added
  "matches_acted_on": 0.41,
  "warmth_alive_21d": 0.55       // the honest judge
}
```

## 7. Acceptance criteria

1. No exit survey form exists. No rating scales anywhere.
2. No feedback is incentivized. No raffles, no gift cards, no rewards for answering.
3. Event health metrics in §1 are derived from behavior with zero questions asked.
4. `connected_zero` is surfaced prominently to the operator. It is the headline metric.
5. Warmth at 21 days is reported per event as the value measure, not night-of sentiment.
6. Every micro-ask is one question, one tap, skippable, and never repeated if ignored.
7. The recap is framed as the person's own memory, and every item passes the "would they do it anyway" test.
8. Qualitative follow-up is a scheduled human activity (calls), not an automated survey.
9. A text path is present wherever a voice path is present. Typing is never hidden behind a failure.
10. With the microphone denied, the recap remains fully usable via text, with no error screen.
11. With the model unavailable, spoken or typed words are still saved as a plain note; only chips are absent.
12. Every recap item is skippable. Nothing is required.

---

## 8. The through-line

SODA's whole premise is that it notices what happened in the room so people do not have to. Applying that to feedback is just consistency.

**A product that remembers connections for you should not need to ask you what happened.**
