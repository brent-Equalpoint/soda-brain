## 📖 Full one-pager (the story)

*An idea goes from a sentence you say out loud to live, tested, and on the site — with a paper trail
the whole way. You stay the decision-maker; the team does the reading, building, testing, and
remembering.*

### The cast
- 🧭 **The Orchestrator** — the lead. Takes your request, runs the show, calls in specialists.
- 🔍 **The Explorers** — read the existing app and your design notes before anything is touched.
- 📐 **The Planners** — design the approach, weigh the options, surface the real decisions.
- 🔨 **The Builders** — write the code to your locked design system and brand rules.
- 🤖 **The Tester (a robot)** — runs the automatic checks and walks the app like a real guest.
- 📚 **The Record-keeper** — keeps the log, the backlog, the flows map, and a printable status report.

### The journey

**1 · You just talk.** You describe what you want in plain English — *"our front door failed, people
got stuck."* No tickets, no specs. That's the whole input.

**2 · The team looks before it leaps.** Before writing a thing, the Explorers read the live app and
your designs to find what's really going on — so the fix is built on reality, not a guess. *(Here:
three dead-ends where a returning guest got trapped.)*

**3 · It plans, and checks with you.** For anything beyond a tiny tweak, the Planners lay out the
approach and ask the questions that matter — *"the recap could show matches, but guests can't see
those yet — skip it or build it?"* You steer before a line of code exists. Nothing big without your yes.

**4 · It builds — on brand.** The Builders write the code to your exact design system: the colors,
fonts, button behavior, the rules. It comes out looking like SODA, not a developer's interpretation.

**5 · A robot proves it works.** Before anything is "done," it's checked, built like the real site, run
through a battery of tests, and a **robot walks through the app on a phone** — signs up, enters a room,
leaves — like a real guest. If a step breaks, it stops and shows a screenshot. Nothing ships broken.

**6 · It ships safely, on your word.** Verified work waits on a safe branch and only goes live when you
say go. Even then, risky things — a database change, an unfinished screen — are **deliberately held
back**, so the live site only ever gets finished, safe work. Every release is clean and reversible.

**7 · It writes everything down.** The Record-keeper keeps a running **day log** (the story), a
**backlog** (everything open, who owns it), a **flows map** (every screen), and a printable **status
report**. Nothing lives only in a chat — you can show anyone exactly where things stand.

**↑ And it scales when the problem is big.** For a hard question, the Orchestrator spins up a whole
crew at once — Explorers searching different corners, Planners designing competing approaches, a
skeptic pressure-testing the result — then brings back one clear answer.

### The result
In a single working stretch this week, that journey took a broken front door, a faster card editor, a
"leave the room" flow, a missing email column, and a host tool — **from sentences to live on
grabsoda.app, tested and recorded** — with the founder steering every release.
