Layer two · the connection

Connection is about pairs. Pairs scale quadratically.

Soda does not manage 100 people. It reads every possible relationship between them, in both directions, in real time.

Possible connections in a room
N × (N − 1) ÷ 2
100 people = 4,950 unique pairs to evaluate

Each pair carries its own match score. Soda checks both directions: does A offer what B needs, does B offer what A needs, is it mutual.

At 100 users that is 4,950 relationships read in real time, and roughly 30,000 overlap computations to surface the matches.

A 100-person event generates ~35,000 discrete points of relational intelligence in one night.

The scaling curve

Ten times the room. One hundred times the connections.

The base data grows in a straight line. The relationship surface explodes. This gap is the entire thesis.
