Onboarding Flowmap

One question gets someone in the room. Everything after that is how the room learns what they offer and who they trust, so context builds instead of getting lost between meetings. One required gate, two optional sheets, and three moments where the system quietly offers help instead of asking for it.

How to read this: the only hard requirement in the whole flow is picking one Entry chip. Everything after that is optional and reversible. The two banners and the toast aren't destinations someone navigates to on purpose; they're the room noticing what's changed and offering the next thoughtful step, the same plain-before-powerful logic that should run through every Equalpoint surface. The match-moment banner in particular fires once, 900ms after an offer is added, as long as no focus has been added yet: the system's one quiet attempt to turn a general offer into something specific before the moment passes.
