Every event has a window. It opens when people walk in. It closes when they leave.

Inside that window, the right connection either happens or it does not.

Most of the time it does not. Not because the right people were not there. Because nothing in the room was built to bring them together.

A name tag does not know who you need. A sign-in sheet has no memory. Soda is the system.

The three acts

Three ways Soda moves a room.

Run one. Run all three. Each does something specific to the energy in the room.

Act 01

The
Drop

One prompt fires to every phone in the room at the same instant. Everyone answers. Responses go up on a shared wall, anonymous at first, then the names reveal. The room sees itself.

Act 02

The
Chance

Soda pairs the room at random and hands each pair a conversation starter pulled from that night's answers. A five-minute timer runs. The person you never would have walked up to.

Act 03

The
Nudge

A private match signal sent only to you. No public leaderboard. No announcement. Just a quiet note: someone here can help, and here is why. Find them before you leave.

The real asset

Every event builds a map of itself.

Soda does more than connect people in a single night. Every event produces a relational map of who was in the room, what they needed, and what they offered.

The community builds a memory of itself over time. Event three is smarter than event one. Event ten knows your network better than any spreadsheet could.

That data is the real asset. Soda is the infrastructure layer for community intelligence at live events.
