The four Supabase layers, and why each one reacts

When your crowd grows, it pushes on four different parts of Supabase. Here is what each one is and why it behaves the way it does.
🎚️

Realtime connections

Live phone-to-server links held open during The Drop and the Nudge.
Why it breaks first: this is a hard concurrent cap, 200 free, 500 Pro. Unlike storage, it is not about total volume over time, it is how many phones are connected at the exact same instant. The moment your event size passes the cap, the next phone literally cannot connect and misses The Drop live in the room.
EFFECT → caps the size of a single event. The binding constraint.
💾

Database storage

Where chips, profiles, Drop answers, and survey responses are saved.
Why it almost never breaks: text is tiny. A person's entire profile and survey history is about 3.6 KB. Even all 8,429 of your emails converting and attending stores around 31 MB against a 500 MB free limit. This is your richest data and your cheapest to keep. It accumulates over time, not per event.
EFFECT → essentially never forces an upgrade. Decades of runway.
📡

Egress (bandwidth out)

Data sent from Supabase out to phones: room views and avatar images.
Why it sneaks up on you: it is monthly volume, not concurrent. Survey and chip text going out is feather-light. Photos are the weight. Many phones loading many avatars repeatedly is what burns the 5 GB free allowance, and a single large event can blow it in one night. Fixable with image compression and caching.
EFFECT → the second wall, driven by photos. Watch as you grow.
👤

Monthly active users (MAU)

Unique people who authenticate (scan in and verify) in a calendar month.
Why it is a non-issue for you: the free plan includes 50,000 MAU. Your entire email list is 8,429. Even at 100% conversion every month you use a sixth of the free allowance. Only people who actually log in count, not stored emails, so your list size does not touch this at all.
EFFECT → no pressure until tens of thousands attend monthly.
